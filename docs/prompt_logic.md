1. Clear business type
Example: Local cricket fitness academy
Why?
Tells the AI what kind of business it is
Helps generate relevant content, services, and wording
Avoids generic or wrong information
Without this, the AI may create content for the wrong niche.
Example:
A cricket academy website sounds very different from a restaurant or gym website.
2. Defined target audience
Example: Teenagers and young adults interested in cricket training and fitness
Why?
Helps the writing speak to the right people
Changes language, style, and messaging
Makes content more relatable and persuasive
Example:
For teenagers → energetic, motivational language
For parents → trust, safety, coaching quality
3. Specified output structure
Example:
Homepage headline
Hero section description
About us section
Services page content
Why?
Gives organized output
Saves time editing
Makes the website content complete and professional
Instead of random paragraphs, you get ready-to-use website sections.
4. Defined tone
Example: Professional, energetic, beginner-friendly, conversion-focused
Why?
Controls how the writing sounds
Keeps brand voice consistent
Example:
Professional → trustworthy
Energetic → exciting and motivating
Beginner-friendly → easy to understand
Conversion-focused → encourages sign-ups/calls
5. Conversion-focused website writing
Why?
The goal of a business website is not only to inform people — it should encourage action.
That means content is written to make visitors:
Join a class
Contact the academy
Book a demo session
Sign up
Example:
Bad:
“We provide cricket training.”
Better (conversion-focused):
“Train with expert coaches, improve your performance, and take your cricket fitness to the next level — enroll today.”
