1. Clear business type
Example: “Local cricket fitness academy”
This tells the AI what business it is writing for. Without this, the content may become too generic or unrelated.
For example:
“Fitness academy” → might give gym content
“Cricket fitness academy” → gives cricket-focused training, batting, stamina, agility, sports performance content
2. Defined target audience
Example: “Teenagers and young adults interested in cricket training and fitness”
This helps match the language, tone, and messaging to the right people.
For example:
Teens → energetic, motivational, beginner-friendly
Parents → safety, trust, discipline
Professionals → performance, advanced coaching
So the website feels like it speaks to the right audience.
3. Specified output structure
Example:
Homepage headline
Hero section
About us
Services
Why choose us
CTA
Contact section
This gives the AI a clear format to follow instead of random content.
Without structure → messy output
With structure → organized, website-ready content
4. Defined tone
Example: Professional, energetic, beginner-friendly, conversion-focused
Tone controls how the writing sounds.
Professional → trustworthy
Energetic → sporty and exciting
Beginner-friendly → easy to understand
Conversion-focused → encourages users to join/contact
Without tone instructions, the content may sound boring, robotic, or inconsistent.
5. Conversion-focused website writing
This means the content should not only inform people but also encourage action (joining, calling, registering, messaging).
Example:
❌ Normal writing:
“हम provide cricket training.”
✅ Conversion-focused writing:
“Start your cricket fitness journey today with expert coaching designed to improve stamina, strength, and match performance.”
It makes visitors more likely to become customers.
